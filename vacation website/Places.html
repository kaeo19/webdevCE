The Citys of Japan

Popular Destinations

Tokyo
The bustling capital city of Japan, known for its vibrant culture, modern skyscrapers, and historic temples.
Home to world-class shopping, dining, and entertainment in neighborhoods like Shibuya, Shinjuku, and Asakusa.
The iconic Senso-ji Temple, Meiji Shrine, and the famous Shibuya Crossing are must-see attractions.
Perfect for experiencing both traditional and contemporary Japanese culture in one dynamic city.
Kyoto
Japan's ancient capital, famous for its thousands of temples, traditional wooden houses, and beautiful gardens.
The serene bamboo groves of Arashiyama and the golden Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) are world-renowned attractions.
Visitors can stroll through the historic Gion district where geisha can still be spotted in the evening.
A peaceful escape from the modern world with rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty.
Mount Fuji and Hakone
Japan's most iconic landmark, Mount Fuji, stands at 3,776 meters and is visible from Tokyo on clear days.
The surrounding Hakone area offers hot springs (onsen), scenic Lake Ashi, and beautiful hiking trails.
Climbers attempt the peak during the official climbing season from July to early September.
An essential destination for experiencing Japan's natural beauty and traditional mountain culture.
Osaka
Known as the "Kitchen of Japan," Osaka is famous for its delicious street food and vibrant nightlife.
Osaka Castle stands as a symbol of the city's rich history and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.
The energetic Dotonbori district is filled with restaurants, shops, and entertainment venues.
A great base for exploring the Kansai region and experiencing authentic Japanese hospitality and cuisine.
Hiroshima
A city of significant historical and cultural importance, dedicated to peace and reconciliation.
The Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum educate visitors about the impact of war and the importance of peace.
The nearby island of Miyajima is home to the stunning Itsukushima Shrine with its famous floating torii gate.
A moving and educational destination that offers both somber reflection and beautiful natural scenery.
Nara
A compact city famous for its sacred deer that roam freely through Nara Park among ancient temples.
Todai-ji Temple houses a massive bronze statue of Buddha and is one of Japan's most significant religious sites.
The quiet, walkable nature of the city makes it perfect for a peaceful day trip from Kyoto or Osaka.
Visitors can interact with friendly deer while exploring centuries-old temples and cultural landmarks.
